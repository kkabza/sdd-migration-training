Not every migration needs the same level of specification. The amount of spec work varies by migration strategy, and understanding this helps you invest effort where it matters most.

At one end of the spectrum, retiring an application needs no spec work — just the decision rationale. Retaining requires minimal documentation of the current state. Rehosting needs a light touch — infrastructure mapping and a dependency list. Replatforming requires medium depth, focusing on the platform-specific adaptation points.

Then we get to where spec-driven migration really shines. Refactoring requires a full behavior spec plus architecture redesign. Re-architecting goes even deeper — domain decomposition and a new architecture. Rebuilding needs a full behavioral specification — what the system does, not how, because the implementation is entirely new. And replacing with a SaaS product requires a gap analysis — what the SaaS covers versus what needs custom work.

The decision process is straightforward: run a lightweight spec pass first to understand complexity. Then choose the migration strategy based on evidence. Trying to choose the strategy before understanding the system is a common anti-pattern that leads to costly mid-migration strategy changes.

You can't confidently choose the right strategy until you've specified what the system actually does. The spec informs the strategy — not the other way around.
