Welcome to Northwind Global Services. Here is the code repository. Here is a one-page README from twenty-nineteen. The previous architect left the company. Good luck.

That is your starting point. Rather than isolated labs, the entire training is structured around a single progressive simulation carried across all three days. You receive a legacy application now and work it through the full spec-driven migration pipeline.

By this point in Module 1, every student should already know that SpecKit is the required path and what artifacts come next. This exercise puts that knowledge into practice for the first time.

Students must accomplish four things in this exercise. First, initialize the working environment without getting blocked on setup. Second, create the first reusable migration artifacts. Third, practice disciplined observation instead of jumping straight to cloud design. And fourth, produce a first-pass assessment that another team could review.

The Northwind codebase includes models, services, controllers, and data layers of a real MVC application. It also contains batch jobs, config helpers, and utility classes with hidden operational behavior. You will find UNC paths, NTLM authentication, EDI exports, and SMTP dependencies. There is enough ambiguity here to simulate a realistic brownfield modernization start.

Why is it hard? Business logic is scattered across Services, Utilities, and Config. Hardcoded Windows paths point to infrastructure you do not control. Authentication and session handling are legacy and brittle. And nobody fully documented the scheduled jobs or their downstream consequences.

Here are the exercise steps. Clone the repository and build it locally. Initialize the SpecKit migration workspace. Write the first migration constitution. Then explore the codebase for twenty minutes, starting with Models, Services, and BatchJobs.

Use the first impressions template. What is immediately clear about the domain? What is unclear, suspicious, or fragile? Where does business logic appear to live? What external integrations can you identify quickly? And what is your initial risk assessment for migration?

The point of the debrief is not to reward guessing. It is to show how much every team misses when analysis is ad hoc, and why systematic reverse engineering begins in Module 2.
